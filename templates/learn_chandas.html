Learn Sanskrit Chandas

Chandas is the study of poetic meter in Sanskrit. It is one of the six Vedāṅgas, the auxiliary disciplines developed to preserve and understand the Vedas.

What is Chandas?

Chandas deals with the rhythm, structure, and measurement of Sanskrit verses. A verse is not only meaningful through words, but also through its sound pattern.

In Sanskrit poetry, this rhythm is created through the arrangement of short and long syllables.

Laghu and Guru

Every syllable, or akṣara, can be classified as either Laghu or Guru.

Laghu means light or short. It takes one mātrā of time.

Guru means heavy or long. It takes two mātrās of time.

Akṣara and Mātrā

An akṣara is roughly a syllable. A mātrā is a unit of time used to measure how long a syllable takes to pronounce.

Laghu syllables take one mātrā, while Guru syllables take two mātrās.

Why Chandas Matters

  • It gives Sanskrit poetry rhythm and beauty.
  • It helps in memorization of verses.
  • It helps detect errors in chanting or recitation.
  • It connects ancient Indian knowledge with mathematics and computer science.

Connection with Binary System

Since every syllable has two possibilities, Laghu or Guru, a Chandas pattern naturally behaves like a binary sequence.

Example:

L G G L

becomes:

1 0 0 1

This is why Pingala's Chandaḥśāstra is important in the history of combinatorics and binary representation.
