Mātrā Chandaḥ Algorithm

Mātrā Chandaḥ asks this question: how many Chandas patterns can be formed with a total mātrā value of m?

This is different from counting patterns of a fixed number of akṣaras. Here, the total time-value matters.

In this app: Laghu = 1 mātrā Guru = 2 mātrās

Core Idea

A pattern of total mātrā m can end in two ways:

  • It can end with a Laghu. Then the remaining mātrā is m - 1.
  • It can end with a Guru. Then the remaining mātrā is m - 2.

Therefore:

M(m) = M(m - 1) + M(m - 2)

This gives a Fibonacci-style sequence, also connected with Virahāṅka and Hemacandra numbers.

Example

For m = 3, the possible patterns are:

  • LLL = 1 + 1 + 1 = 3 mātrās
  • GL = 2 + 1 = 3 mātrās
  • LG = 1 + 2 = 3 mātrās

Therefore: M(3) = 3
